# ArkyHub BrandScript — StoryBrand SB7 Framework

Based on Donald Miller's "Building a StoryBrand" — 7 elements applied to ArkyHub.

---

## 1. CHARACTER — The Hero & Their Desire

**Hero:** The leader of an architecture firm — whether the principal architect, the project manager, or the firm owner.

**What they want:** Deliver projects with full control, knowing that every stakeholder has the correct information at all times.

> *"Deliver every project with confidence — knowing everyone has the right information at the right time."*

---

## 2. PROBLEM — Three Levels + Villain

### The Villain

**Fragmentation** — the disconnected tools that scatter project information across platforms, inboxes, and chat threads.

### External Problem (the tangible issue)

Project documents are spread across email, cloud drives, WhatsApp, and tools that don't talk to each other. Nobody knows which version is the latest. Contractors work from outdated drawings. Clients can't get a clear status update.

### Internal Problem (the emotional frustration)

- The anxiety of not knowing whether the contractor has the correct plans
- The frustration of looking disorganized in front of a client who asks for an update and you can't respond quickly
- The feeling that the team spends more time managing files than actually designing

### Philosophical Problem (what ought to be)

Professionals shouldn't waste their talent managing document chaos. Every stakeholder deserves access to the truth of a project.

> *"It shouldn't take more effort to find a document than to create it."*

---

## 3. GUIDE — Empathy + Authority

### Empathy

"We know what it's like to search for the right version of a drawing while the client is waiting for an answer. We know how it feels to discover the contractor built from a previous version."

### Authority

ArkyHub is the first product from Arkytech — a company created to digitalize the AEC sector. Built specifically for how architecture firms work, not adapted from another industry.

## 4. PLAN — Three Simple Steps

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | **Create your account** and set up your firm in minutes |
| 2 | **Upload your first project** — plans (DWG or PDF), BIM models, and virtual tours |
| 3 | **Invite your team members, contractors, and stakeholders** — each person sees only what they need |

---

## 5. CALL TO ACTION

**Direct CTA:** "Join the Early Access List"
→ Bright, primary button. Visible above the fold and repeated throughout the page.

**Transitional CTA:** "Schedule a Demo"
→ Secondary button, placed next to the direct CTA. Lower commitment, builds relationship.

Both CTAs work together — Miller recommends exactly this pairing.

## 6. FAILURE — What's at Stake

Without a centralized system:

- A contractor builds from a previous version — rework that costs thousands and delays weeks
- The client loses trust because nobody can give them a clear project status
- The team spends more time searching for files than designing
- A version error in a compliance review can escalate into a legal problem

### Validated Industry Data

| Stat | Source |
|------|--------|
| **$177.5 billion** lost annually in the U.S. in labor costs due to rework, searching for project data, and conflict resolution | PlanGrid & FMI Corporation, *"Construction Disconnected"* report, 2018 — survey of ~600 construction leaders |
| **48% of all rework** on U.S. construction jobsites is caused by poor communication and poor project data | PlanGrid & FMI Corporation, *"Construction Disconnected"* report, 2018 |
| **22% of rework** is due to inaccurate or inaccessible project information — representing **$31.3 billion** in annual rework costs | PlanGrid & FMI Corporation, *"Construction Disconnected"* report, 2018 — FMI estimate based on survey data |
| Construction professionals spend **over 14 hours per week** on non-optimal tasks like searching for files and reconciling outdated documents | PlanGrid & FMI Corporation, *"Construction Disconnected"* report, 2018 |
| Rework accounts for **5–10% of total project cost** on average, with some studies reporting up to 20% | Construction Industry Institute (CII); PlanRadar *"Construction QA/QC Impact Report"*, 2025 — survey of 811 professionals across 13 countries |
| Each **$1 invested** in documentation quality management generates an average return of **$7.40** in prevented rework and delay costs | Hwang et al. (2014) — study across 103 infrastructure projects, cited in *Journal of Construction Engineering and Management* |
| Bad data in construction caused an estimated **$1.8 trillion in losses worldwide** in 2020 and was responsible for **14% of avoidable rework** ($88 billion) | Autodesk & FMI Consulting, *"Harnessing the Data Advantage in Construction"* report, 2021 — survey of 3,900 construction professionals |

## 7. SUCCESS — The Transformation

### Before

- Multiple disconnected tools
- Version confusion across the team
- Contractors working from obsolete documents
- Clients asking "how's my project going?" with no fast answer
- Stress and isolation — team members hoard information to avoid chaos

### After

- One dashboard where you open any project and see every plan, every 3D model, every issue — updated, version-controlled, accessible
- The client logs in and sees their project without asking you for anything
- The contractor always has the latest approved drawing
- Issues are tracked with precise references to the exact plan version
- The team focuses on design, not file management

> *"One workspace. Every document. Every stakeholder. Always current."*

## One-Liner (Elevator Pitch)

**Problem:** Architecture teams waste hours managing documents across disconnected tools — and when the wrong version reaches a job site, it costs thousands.

**Solution:** ArkyHub centralizes every project document in one workspace with version control, BIM visualization, and role-based access for every team member, contractor, and stakeholder.

**Result:** Teams deliver projects faster, clients stay informed, and contractors always build from the right plans.

## Website Above the Fold — Checklist (Chapter 12)

Per StoryBrand, the website must include:

1. ✅ **An offer above the fold** — the one-liner or a clear aspirational statement
2. ✅ **Obvious calls to action** — "Join the Early Access List" (bright) + "Schedule a Demo" (secondary)
3. ✅ **Images of success** — happy architects reviewing plans on a clean dashboard, not stock photos of buildings
4. ✅ **Bite-sized breakdown** — the 3-step plan visible without scrolling far
5. ✅ **Very few words** — write in Morse code, not paragraphs
